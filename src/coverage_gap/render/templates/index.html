Why this exists

Sixty million Americans in rural areas face severe specialist shortages: 30 physicians per 100,000 people versus 263 in urban areas. The institutions making 5 to 30 year decisions about physician supply, medical schools setting residency counts, federal programs allocating workforce funding, hospital systems planning specialty departments, work from data that is years stale and aggregated to the state level. Tenens Health is building the granular real-time signal those institutions need. This index is the first artifact in that effort.

How to read this map

Each Mississippi county is shaded by the worst hospital gap count it contains: the highest number of HIGH or CRITICAL specialty gaps among the Critical Access Hospitals inside that county. {{ counties_with_data }} of {{ counties_count }} counties contain a federally designated CAH; the rest are shown in gray.

The dots on the map are the {{ cah_count }} CAHs themselves, colored by the same severity scale at the per-hospital level. Click any dot to see the full specialty breakdown for that hospital.
